About

Graduated from Cal Poly San Luis Obispo with a Bachelor’s Degree in Civil Engineering. I worked in the construction industry for 2.4 years as a Project Engineer. I learned a lot of skills while working in the industry, but decided that I wanted to make a career change. Reason being that I have been trying to find my passion in life. I thought it was Civil Engineering because I enjoyed the challenge that problem solving brought and understanding the ingenuity behind it. Don’t get me wrong I enjoyed my education and if I had to choose again. I would choose the same. I had various internships while studying, but none of them were something that I wanted to continue doing after I graduated.

After I graduated I worked in the construction industry as a Project Engineer, and enjoyed learning what it means to manage a job site. While in the industry I worked in two construction companies where I saw how they could potentially be improve by technology/applications. I decided to take a course in coding to learn more about artificial intelligence. My goal was to make a program to help automate a some of the mundane work which Project Engineers need to do. This would help by freeing up time, which can then be directing towards more important tasks.

I began my education in coding and I feel in love. The learning and creating aspect drew me to the point where I wanted to change my career. Taking the course while working was a challenge given that my work demanded most of my time, but I dedicated my weekends to trying to master a new skill which challenged me. While frustrating at times seeing the end product was always worth it.

The key reason to why I loved Civil Engineering was seeing how the whole structure is being built. Learning how all the different trades work/communicate together in order to build it. Coding is no different I love planning, creating, executing, and resolving unanticipated issues in the code. Seeing the end result gives a sense of pride and gratitude.
